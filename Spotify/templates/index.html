Music Taste and Machine Learning

Background:

Spotify is a music and podcast streaming platform founded in 2006. It is one of the most popular streaming services in the world. As of the final quarter of 2018, Spotify had 96 million premium subscribers worldwide.* An essential ingredient in Spotify's success is the 'recommended music' functionality. Every Monday, Spotify provides its premium users with a playlist of recommended songs, based on an individual's listening habits.



Research Objective:

Simulate Spotify's 'recommended music' functionality with machine learning to predict whether or not a Spotify user will like a playlist, as determined by their listening habits.

Methods:

We will pull listener data from three Spotify listeners' "Top Songs 2018" playlists. Data is broken down with Spotify's "Sort Your Music" functionality. Sort Your Music provides a data frame for determining music taste with several metrics:

  • Tempo: Measured in beats per minute.
  • Energy: The energy of a song - the higher the value, the more energetic.
  • Loudness: The higher the value, the louder the song.
  • Valence: The higher the value, the more positive mood for the song.

Workflow:

  1. Scrape data from Spotify's "Global Top 50" playlist and three users' "Your Top Songs 2018" playlist using "Sort Your Music" and Spotipy, a lightweight Python library for the Spotify Web API.
  2. Import and clean data in a dataframe using Pandas; visualize initial charts with MatPlotLib.
  3. Identify the user's optimal song type using "Sort Your Music" data aggregation system.
  4. Develop two predictive models with ScitKitLearn to run an analysis identifying whether or not a user will like a playlist.
  5. Visualize results with D3.

*Source: Statista
